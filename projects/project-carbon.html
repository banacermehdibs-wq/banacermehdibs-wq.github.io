Spanderswoudstraat Carbon Study

Year 2024
Role Visualization & Analysis
Location Almere, NL
Tools Excel, Illustrator, SVG

A scenario-based analysis of embodied and operational carbon across four renovation strategies for an Ymere social housing block.

This study compared four renovation scenarios for a 1980s social housing building in Almere — ranging from full demolition and rebuild to a biobased deep-renovation strategy. The goal was to give the client and design team a clear, visual basis for decision-making rather than a dense spreadsheet of figures.

Approach

Embodied carbon was calculated per building element (facade, roof, floor, foundations) under both conventional and biobased insulation assumptions. Results were modelled cumulatively over a 47-year horizon to show when each strategy's carbon investment is repaid through reduced operational emissions.

Outcome

The biobased renovation scenario reached net-negative embodied carbon within the first year, while demolition carried an upfront penalty of over 6,000 tonnes CO₂. The renovated building also achieved an 89% reduction in operational heat loss compared to its current state. The visualizations were delivered as a set of SVGs for direct use in the project's Figma and Illustrator deliverables.
